# Reading Notes: Stark — Preface

<!-- p. xi -->
**Core Thesis:**
Stark proposes a "rapprochement" between historical and scientific views of singing.
> "The critical method is to seek a rapprochement between historical and scientific views of good singing - that is, to extrapolate backwards in time our current understanding of the physiology, aerodynamics, and acoustics of singing as a means of reinterpreting historical vocal practices."

**Key Quote:**
> "Understanding takes place in the fusion of the horizons of present and past." — Leo Treitler (1982, 71)

**Focal Point:**
The method of **Manuel Garcia II** (mid-19th century) is identified as the watershed between tradition and science.

<!-- p. xii -->
**Structure of the Book:**
- **Chapters 1–5:** Specific technical concepts (glottal closure, timbre, registers, breath support, vibrato) examined historically and scientifically.
- **Chapter 6:** Idiomatic techniques and expression of the Old Italian School.
- **Chapter 7:** Definition of *bel canto* and its historical context.
- **Appendix:** Report on experiments at the Groningen Voice Research Lab (Schutte & Miller).

**Scope & Limitations:**
- **Starts:** Late 16th/early 17th century (emergence of solo virtuoso repertoire).
- **Ignores:** Pre-professional era, idiosyncratic authors.
