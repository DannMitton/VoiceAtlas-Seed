## Role in Voice Atlas

Fields is a cataloguing source, not a claims source. Its value to Atlas is
structural:

- Establishes the nine-area taxonomy that Monahan and Burgin will echo
- Provides the first systematic attempt to count and classify pedagogy claims
  in English-language literature (702 sources, 2,946 concepts)
- Identifies 23 controversial questions still unresolved as of 1947
- Provides 177 working definitions of vocal terms
- Documents a fifteen-year bibliographic period (approximately 1930–1945)

Claims drawn from Fields, Monahan, or Burgin require follow-up to the
primary sources they cite before reaching Atlas staging. These books describe
the map of the territory, not the territory itself.

## The Nine-Area Taxonomy

Fields organises all of vocal pedagogy into nine areas. He states explicitly
that "the first of these nine divisions provides a general approach to the
subject; the other eight may be considered components of the act of singing"
(p. 11). Together they constitute "an organic configuration that encompasses
all the primary pedagogical aspects of the singer's art."

1. **Pedagogy** — general principles and methods of teaching singing
2. **Breathing** — activation and control of the respiratory organs
3. **Phonation** — inception of vibratory activity in the glottis
4. **Resonance** — accessory vibration that amplifies and enriches the voice
5. **Range** — extent of the musical scale between lowest and highest pitches
6. **Dynamics** — variation and control of loudness and carrying power
7. **Ear Training** — developing hearing acuity for vocal sounds
8. **Diction** — enunciation and verbal intelligibility in vocal expression
9. **Interpretation** — communication of mood and thought values in singing

**Claim:** Fields's nine-area taxonomy represents the first systematic attempt
to organise English-language vocal pedagogy into discrete, exhaustive
categories.

## The 23 Controversial Questions (Table Eleven)

Fields's own identification of unresolved disputes in the 1947 literature.
Each represents a live debate, not a settled claim. Atlas status for all:
*Contested* as of 1947. Post-1947 science has resolved some, complicated
others, and left several still open. Priority nodes for cross-referencing
with Stark and the acoustic generation.

**Pedagogy:**
1. Vocal teaching can / cannot be standardised
2. Technique should / should not be acquired through songs

**Breathing:**
3. Direct control of breathing organs is / is not advised
4. Diaphragmatic control is / is not essential

**Phonation:**
5. Mouth opening is / is not important in phonation
6. Low tongue is desirable / free tongue is desirable
7. Palate should be raised / should be free
8. Conscious control of throat is / is not desirable
9. Larynx should / should not move during phonation

**Resonance:**
10. Head cavities are / are not important as resonators
11. Sinuses are / are not used as resonators
12. Nasal cavities are / are not consciously employed
13. Vocal resonance is / is not directly controllable
14. The voice should / should not be consciously focussed
15. Humming is / is not a useful device

**Range:**
16. The singing voice has one / two / three registers
17. Falsetto tones are / are not legitimate
18. Use the entire vocal range / use only middle range in beginning practice

**Dynamics:**
19. Loud tones should / should not be used when practising

**Ear Training:**
20. Self-listening is / is not recommended
21. Sensation is / is not a reliable vocal aid (third option: sound and
    sensation combined are reliable)

**Diction:**
22. Vowels should / should not be altered on high pitches

**Interpretation:**
23. Foreign language study is / is not essential for singers

## Structural Claim

**Claim:** Fields explicitly positions his work as a prerequisite to further
research rather than a contribution to claims: "The work of classifying the
known theories and teaching concepts must precede any further research in
this area if serious blundering and repetitious effort are to be avoided"
(p. 1). This is the founding rationale of the Cataloguing Generation.
**Type:** Interpretive Synthesis
**Status:** V0
**AtlasNode:** Pedagogy

**Kernel:** Nobody had counted the claims before Fields did. That act of
counting is itself a claim about the state of the field.
