# Reading Notes: Stark Ch. 1 — The Coup de la Glotte

<!-- p. 3 -->
**Subject:** Manuel Garcia II (1805–1906).
**Thesis:** Garcia represents the watershed where tradition and science collided. He combined the old Italian school with empirical observation (laryngoscope).

**Biography:**
- Born Madrid 1805. Father: Manuel Garcia I (Rossini's tenor). Mother: Maria Joaquina Sitches (singer/dancer).
- Sisters: Maria Malibran, Pauline Viardot (famous mezzo-sopranos).
- Studied voice with father, harmony with Fétis. Possible lessons with Ansani (student of Porpora).
- **Career Shift:** Sang in NY/Mexico (1825). Voice damaged by puberty/overwork. Stopped performing 1829.
- Worked in military hospitals studying larynx in neck wounds.
- Took over teaching sisters (1832); they became successful.
- Appointed Paris Conservatoire Professor (1835).

<!-- p. 4 -->
**The Paris Context:**
- Conservatoire used Italian methods (Mengozzi/Garaudé) based on Bernacchi/Crescentini.
- Directed by Cherubini (Italian school sanctioned).
- **Garcia's Breakthrough (1840):** Presented *Mémoire sur la voix humaine* to Académie des Sciences.
- Published *École de Garcia* (1841). Reprinted 1847 (2 parts).
  - Part 1: Technique.
  - Part 2: Stylistic practices.
- **Citation Note:** Garcia acknowledged earlier treatises (Tosi, Mancini, etc.) but noted they were "vague and incomplete."

<!-- p. 5 -->
**The Laryngoscope:**
- **Invention:** 1855. Inspired by sun reflection off palace window.
- **Precedents:** Bozzini (1807), Gagniard de la Tour (1829), Avery (1844) used mirrors. Garcia likely first for *autolaryngoscopy*.
- **Publication:** "Observations on the Human Voice" (Royal Society, 1855).
- **Limitations:** Epiglottis often blocked view of anterior third of glottis, especially in chest voice.
- **Impact:** Became chief diagnostic tool in laryngology.
- **Later Life:** *Hints on Singing* (1894) defended his theories. Taught until 1895 (Royal Academy). Died 1906 (age 101).

**Notable Pupils:**
- Sisters: Malibran, Viardot.
- Others: Mathilde Marchesi, Charles Battaille, Julius Stockhausen, Santley, Johanna Wagner, Jenny Lind.
- Marchesi taught Calvé, Eames, Melba.
- Stockhausen taught Goldschmidt, Friedlaender.

<!-- p. 7 -->
**Anatomy of the Larynx:**
- **Cartilages:** Cricoid (base), Arytenoids (paired, pyramid-shaped, move on cricoid), Thyroid (shield-shaped, Adam's apple).
- **Muscles:**
  - *Intrinsic:* Move cartilages relative to each other (control glottis).
  - *Extrinsic:* Move entire larynx up/down.
- **Glottis:** Variable opening between vocal folds.
  - *Membranous portion:* Anterior 3/5. Active in vibration.
  - *Cartilaginous portion:* Posterior 2/5. Can be adducted or fixed.
