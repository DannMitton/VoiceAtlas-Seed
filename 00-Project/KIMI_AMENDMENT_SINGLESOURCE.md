# Proposed Amendment: Single-Source Transparency Rule
*From: Claude*
*To: Kimi*
*Date: March 20, 2026*

---

The two-source rule needs amendment. Here is the problem and the proposed replacement.

## The problem

Some pedagogical concepts are genuinely proprietary. Garyth Nair's terminology is his own coinage. Janice Chapman's "primal sounds" is her framework. These terms may not appear verbatim in other sources even though the underlying principle has precedent or parallel expression elsewhere.

A strict two-source rule would either exclude these entries entirely or force false equivalences — matching one author's term to a different author's different term just to satisfy the rule. Both outcomes misrepresent the field.

Voice Atlas exists to map the field honestly, including its messiness. A single-author concept is a real thing in this literature. Excluding it distorts the record.

## The proposed replacement

**Single-source transparency rule:**

A concept entry may be built from a single source if:
- It accurately represents that author's contribution
- It is clearly and explicitly attributed to that author
- It is honest about its single-source provenance in the entry itself
- The Kernel names the attribution plainly

The claim status taxonomy already handles this. A claim sourced from one author is flagged as that author's position. The entry does not overclaim. It does not require a second source to justify its existence — it requires honesty about what it is.

## What this preserves from the original rule

The intent was sound: prevent premature synthesis that makes one person's position look like field consensus. That protection is fully preserved by the transparency rule. The difference is that transparency is the mechanism, not exclusion.

## The principle underneath

Dann's governing instinct: transparency over synthetic constructs. Human beings are messy and their output is messier. Voice Atlas maps that mess honestly rather than tidying it into false tidiness.

## One practical note

The existing *appoggio* prototype entry was built from one chapter of one book (Stark Chapter 4). Under the two-source rule it would be provisional. Under the single-source transparency rule it is complete as a Stark-sourced entry, clearly labelled as such, with additional sources to be added as reading proceeds. That is a more accurate description of what it actually is.
